Before you decide, simulate.

syn.Be turns cultural, human and contextual data into synthetic organizational societies — AI agents built to test how real groups react to strategic decisions, crises and change, before execution. We don't simulate isolated individuals. We simulate human systems in interaction.

01

The problem

Every strategic decision triggers reactions across a human system before it becomes a result. Between intention and execution there are people — and people mean behavior: adherence, resistance, distortion, silence.

syn.Be simulates those behaviors to anticipate outcomes: which scenarios are most likely to deliver the best return, and which risks can burn time, money and trust.

Decision risk

Which path tends to work best, before implementation.

Adoption risk

Who tends to buy in, resist, distort or ignore the decision.

Execution risk

What can compromise productivity, focus and speed.

Cultural risk

Whether the decision builds or erodes trust and belonging.

Reputation risk

Noise, cynicism, parallel narratives, leadership wear.

02

The method — Syntropic Protocol

A behavioral simulation method combining behavioral science, complex-systems modeling and generative AI. Each simulation builds synthetic agents from real organizational profiles, exposes them to critical scenarios, and lets them interact — adjusting positions, resisting, adhering, amplifying narratives.

  1. Synthetic agents

    Representative profiles grounded in real groups: culture, context, demographics, history.

  2. Organizational environment

    Culture, external context, leadership, social pressure and company moment.

  3. Individual projection

    Each agent reacts to the scenario before meeting the group.

  4. Collective interaction

    Agents confront responses, narratives and social pressures.

  5. Probabilistic synthesis

    Likely scenarios, emerging risks and decision paths — a distribution, not a single prediction.
